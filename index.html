Advice
How to Actually Get In
Straight talk on making the most of these opportunities for college applications
01
Depth beats breadth
One program where you led a project beats five where you just participated. Admissions officers are looking for sustained commitment and real impact, not a long list of activities.
02
Apply to things that seem out of reach
Programs like COSMOS, NASA Scholars, and SSP have sub-15% acceptance rates, but Central Valley students are underrepresented. Geographic diversity works in your favor. Apply anyway.
03
Cold email a professor
UC Merced is 45 minutes from Modesto. Email a faculty member in the MAE department asking to assist in their lab over summer. Most students never try this. It works more often than you'd think.
04
Document everything with numbers
"Taught 3 workshops to 40 students" is more powerful than "volunteered at STEM events." Track attendance, hours, and outcomes from the very first session. You'll thank yourself in October of senior year.
05
Start something, don't just join something
A club you founded tells a story. A club you joined doesn't, unless you eventually led it. Starting a STEM workshop series, a Science Olympiad team, or a GitHub project puts you in a completely different tier.
06
Your location is an asset
The Central Valley is underrepresented at top universities. UC schools actively want geographic diversity. Make your story, "growing up in an agricultural region with limited STEM access", as a strength, not a disadvantage.
